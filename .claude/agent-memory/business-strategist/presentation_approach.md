Structure that works for hotel BI to non-technical executive audiences:

1. Lead with methodology "why" before any numbers — frames the entire presentation and builds trust before data.
2. Name the explainability tradeoff explicitly ("I chose transparency over complexity") — resonates with operational executives who have been burned by black-box models.
3. Plain English before math — state what the formula does in one sentence, then show the formula. Never lead with the formula.
4. Story structure for findings: characterize each hotel's demand profile before citing statistics. "Boston tells a very different story" lands better than leading with 88.7%.
5. Specific dates + dollar amounts make recommendations credible. "July 9–12, $480–$520 ADR target, $76,160 incremental revenue" is more actionable than "raise rates on peak dates."
6. Data audit section should lead with the most dramatic finding (fan-out 6x error) to demonstrate rigor — then move quickly through remaining checks.
7. End with modularity and operationalization — shows the work has a future beyond the interview.

**Why:** This pattern was designed for a Pebblebrook interview with Senior Director of Operational Excellence and Manager of Enterprise AI Solutions. Both are likely to scrutinize data integrity and practical implementability.

**How to apply:** When writing future BI presentations for hotel operations audiences, follow this sequence: Methodology Why → Hotel Story A → Hotel Story B → Specific Date Callouts → Recommendations with dollar impacts → Data Audit (headline finding first) → Forward look.
